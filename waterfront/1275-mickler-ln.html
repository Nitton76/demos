Why “23.9% capacity factor” sounds better than it is: A 20–24% capacity factor implies the plant runs ~1 in 5 hours. Anclote’s actual pattern is near-continuous idling rather than cycling off. Steam plants behave this way because cold-starts are expensive and stressful on equipment. The steam turbine spins at constant 3,600 RPM whether generating 80 MW or 500 MW. The transformer hums at 60 Hz constantly. Low-frequency content is load-independent.
How to test properly:
(1) Tonight at 6 PM = peak load → loudest the plant gets — if you hear it from the back yard, note it.
(2) Return at 3 AM on a normal running night → minimum-load baseline you’d live with every night.
(3) Return during a confirmed maintenance outage → true ambient floor without the plant (only 3 windows in 10 months).
Difference between (2) and (3) = your actual nightly noise exposure.
