The business case

Built for the merchants the financial system left behind.

Two billion adults are unbanked. Billions more run small businesses on currencies that lose value by the month and card rails that take a brutal cut. Cowbolt Register hands every one of them a checkout that costs almost nothing and pays out instantly.
No bank, no terminal
A card machine needs a merchant account, a credit check, and a $200–400 device.
Old way: shut out entirely
Cowbolt: a phone they already own
Fees that eat the margin
Card rails take 3–7% plus monthly terminal fees; cross-border adds FX gouging.
Old way: 5–10% all-in
Cowbolt: ~2%, or $0 on a flat plan
Money held hostage
Banks hold payouts 2–30 days and freeze accounts without warning.
Old way: wait days, risk a freeze
Cowbolt: settles in ~1 second
Currency that melts
In high-inflation economies, today's takings are worth less by next week.
Old way: hold a melting local note
Cowbolt: take USDT, hold dollars

🌎 Why this matters most in poor countries

  • The unbanked get a checkout. No bank account is the norm, not the exception — Cowbolt needs none.
  • Inflation defense built in. Vendors in Argentina, Nigeria, Lebanon, Turkey can keep takings in dollars (USDT) instead of a currency losing 30–100%/yr.
  • Remittances without the 6–10% cut. Family abroad pays a link; it lands in seconds, not days, minus almost nothing.
  • No card infrastructure required. Where Visa/Mastercard acceptance barely exists, a QR on a phone leapfrogs it entirely.
  • Censorship- and seizure-resistant. Self-custody means no government or bank can freeze a small business's cash.
  • Tiny, fast, offline-friendly. The web app is a few KB — it loads on a 2G connection and a $40 handset.
